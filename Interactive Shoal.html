Interactive Shoal

Intro

Interactive Shoal is a project I did in 2014, together with another classmate from Media Technolgoy class at Leiden Univeristy. This project was inpired by the flocking behaviour of animals (e.g. birds, fish) and seeked to explore the relationship between human and nature by enabling interaction between the viewers and the shoal.

I did the major development work for this project, including flocking creation (used opensource flocking simuation), motion detection using Kinect, and interative design.

This project was first exhibited in NMNT 2014 OPEN LAB EXPO, and later in the Night of Art and Science in Leiden.

Project details

The set up of the project consists of a laptop (hidden), a kiinect sensor (on the floor), a projecter (on the floor), and a ceiling where the image of shoal is projected on. The shoal is moving slowly accorss the ceiling, exhibting a mysterious yet fascinating flocking behaviour. When a viewer streches his/her arm above the projector and kinect sensor, the viewer can see the shadow of his/her arm appear on the ceiling, forming a giant "arm" among the shoal. When the viewer moves his/her arm, the shoal will slowly follow and gather around the arm.
